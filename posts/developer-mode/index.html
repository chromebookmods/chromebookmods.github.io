How To Enable Developer Mode on a Chromebook

Enabling Developer Mode is very simple but it will cost your data to go. Make sure to back up your files and everything on the Settings App and put it on Google Drive or on an external drive.
WARNING: BACKUP YOUR DATA


Alright, first what you want to do is press Power Button + ESC + Refresh (F3) and hold it for 3 seconds. You may see this "scary" screen. Just press Ctrl+D.

Images by TheSonicMaster


Once you press Ctrl+D, something like this would show.

Images by TheSonicMaster

Just press the enter key. Your Chromebook will reboot and show you this screen.

Images by TheSonicMaster

Now, press Ctrl+D to enable Developer Mode; it will start clearing the data and powerwashing your Chromebook.

Images by TheSonicMaster

This loading takes around half a minute. Once that is done, the Chromebook would show "Preparing System for Developer Mode". This would take around 5-10 minutes.

Images by TheSonicMaster

Finally, the last screen you will see is under the text. When you see this, just press Ctrl+D.

Images by TheSonicMaster

Everytime you turn on your Chromebook, you will need to press Ctrl+D for it to boot into ChromeOS. To turn Developer Mode off, you can check it on one of the posts in the "Other Mods" section on the navigation bar.
